## Design Context

### Users
Security, compliance, and audit teams use Controls Tracker to coordinate framework assessments, assign owners and reviewers, collect evidence, and monitor overdue or blocked controls. They are typically working in operational contexts where clarity, traceability, and speed matter more than visual novelty.

### Brand Personality
Calm, credible, operational. The interface should reduce anxiety around compliance work, help teams feel in control of audit readiness, and present status in a way that feels dependable rather than flashy.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light-first product UI with restrained contrast, compact density, and clear status emphasis. The visual language should feel more like a serious operations workspace than a startup dashboard. References implied by the product: mature enterprise tools with strong information hierarchy and low ornamentation. Anti-reference: generic AI-generated admin dashboards with decorative KPI hero blocks, interchangeable card grids, or glow-heavy dark themes.

### Design Principles
1. Prioritize immediate task clarity over decorative polish.
2. Make state, responsibility, and next actions visible without requiring exploration.
3. Keep dense information scannable through hierarchy, grouping, and consistent status treatment.
4. Support both frequent operators and first-time reviewers with low-friction navigation and obvious affordances.
5. Use visual restraint to build trust, but avoid generic admin-dashboard sameness.
